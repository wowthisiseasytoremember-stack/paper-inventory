### The "Cheat Sheet" for Comic Books
*   **RULE 1 (Newsstand vs. Direct):** This is critical for 1990s comics. Look at the barcode box on the cover. If there is a UPC barcode with a price, it is a **Newsstand Edition** (often rarer and more valuable in high grade). If there is a strike-through, a picture of Spider-Man/another character, or it says "Direct Edition", it is a **Direct Edition**.
*   **RULE 2 (Key Issues):** Identify "Key Issues" (1st appearances, major deaths, new costumes). If the cover boasts an event (e.g., "1st Full Appearance of Venom"), that is a major value driver.
*   **RULE 3 (Variant Covers):** The 90s were full of gimmicks. Note if the cover is foil, chromium, hologram, die-cut, or a specific artist variant (e.g., Todd McFarlane, Jim Lee).
*   **RULE 4 (Condition is Everything):** Look closely at the spine (spine ticks/color breaks) and corners (blunting). A 1990s comic must be near perfect (NM 9.4+) to have significant value unless it's a major key.
