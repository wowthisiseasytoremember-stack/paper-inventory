### The "Cheat Sheet" for Serial Publications
*   **RULE 1 (The Cover Star/Artist):** The value of a magazine is almost entirely dictated by who or what is on the cover. A 1950s magazine with Marilyn Monroe or a cover illustrated by Norman Rockwell is worth exponentially more than a generic issue from the same year.
*   **RULE 2 (First Issues):** "Volume 1, Number 1" (Premier Issues) are highly collected across almost all titles.
*   **RULE 3 (Niche Subject Matter):** Niche trade journals (e.g., vintage computing, early aviation, specific hobbies) often have higher sell-through rates to dedicated collectors than mass-market general interest magazines (like Life or Time), unless the mass-market one covers a major historical event (e.g., Moon Landing, JFK Assassination).
*   **RULE 4 (Condition):** Missing pages, cut-out ads, and detached covers destroy value. Note spine rolling and address labels printed on the cover (which slightly reduce value but are common).
