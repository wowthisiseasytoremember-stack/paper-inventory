### The "Cheat Sheet" for Postal History
*   **RULE 1 (First Day Covers - FDCs):** These are envelopes bearing a stamp cancelled on its first day of issue. Value depends heavily on the "Cachet" (the artwork on the left side of the envelope) and the cachet maker (e.g., Artcraft, Fleetwood, Crosby).
*   **RULE 2 (Cancellations & Postmarks):** Sometimes the stamp itself is worthless, but the ink cancellation is valuable. Look for "Fancy Cancels" (geometric shapes, stars, animals), "Doane Cancels", or cancellations from dead post offices (Discontinued Post Offices - DPOs), ships, or military bases.
*   **RULE 3 (Condition / Centering):** For loose stamps, centering (the image relative to the perforated edges) and the condition of the perforations are everything. Note if the stamp is "Mint Never Hinged" (MNH - no glue residue on back) or "Used".
*   **RULE 4 (Errors, Freaks, and Oddities):** Look for misaligned colors, missing perforations, or inverted designs. These are incredibly rare and valuable.
