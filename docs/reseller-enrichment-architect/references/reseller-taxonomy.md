# Reseller Taxonomy: Sorting for Profit

When designing the categories for the Router/Conductor, don't group items by what they *are* (e.g., "Paper" vs. "Cardboard"). Group them by **the specific knowledge required to value them**.

Since this is for a single user trying to list items fast, keep the buckets broad but distinct enough that an "Expert Prompt" can give useful pricing advice.

## Recommended Buckets for this Hoard

1.  `vintage_comics`
    *   **The Buyer:** Comic collectors, speculators.
    *   **Value Drivers:** Issue #, Key appearances (1st app), Newsstand vs. Direct, visible spine ticks.
    *   **Expert Focus:** Extracting the exact issue/vol to check Key Collector or eBay sold comps.

2.  `railroadiana`
    *   **The Buyer:** Train buffs, local historians.
    *   **Value Drivers:** Specific rail line (e.g., Denver Rio Grande), year, route maps, employee-only vs. public timetables.
    *   **Expert Focus:** Identifying the exact railway company and the era.

3.  `aerospace_technical`
    *   **The Buyer:** Space program collectors, engineers, historians.
    *   **Value Drivers:** The specific program (Apollo, Shuttle), company (North American Aviation/Rockwell), "Internal Use Only" markings, D-numbers.
    *   **Expert Focus:** Pulling out acronyms and document numbers to research highly obscure, high-value technical manuals.

4.  `philately_and_postal`
    *   **The Buyer:** Stamp collectors.
    *   **Value Drivers:** Cancellation marks, First Day Covers, specific misprints, historical letters.
    *   **Expert Focus:** Identifying the exact country, denomination, and postmark date.

5.  `analog_media`
    *   **The Buyer:** Audiophiles, DJ's, retro tech collectors.
    *   **Value Drivers:** Specific pressing plants, Mono vs. Stereo, white label promos, visible scratches.
    *   **Expert Focus:** Reading the fine print on the center label to identify the exact release version.

6.  `general_vintage_ephemera` (The Catch-All)
    *   **The Buyer:** Junk journalers, decorators, casual collectors.
    *   **Value Drivers:** Aesthetic appeal, recognizable brands (e.g., old Coke ads), local town names.
    *   **Expert Focus:** Generating broad, keyword-stuffed titles and summarizing the visual content.
